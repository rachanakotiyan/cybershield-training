Password Hygiene & Security

Strength vs Complexity

For years, employees were forced to create complex passwords containing symbols and numbers, like Tr0ub4dor&3. While complex, these passwords are short, easily guessed by modern computer scripts, and hard for humans to remember.

The Passphrase Method

Instead of a complex word, choose 4+ random words joined by dashes or spaces (e.g. correct-horse-battery-staple). This sequence is longer (length is the most critical password strength indicator) and extremely easy to remember.

Crucial Password Rules

What to Do:

  • Use 14+ characters minimum.
  • Use unique passwords for each account.
  • Use an approved password manager.

What to Avoid:

  • No personal info (names, birth years).
  • Never reuse passwords on personal and corporate portals.
  • Never share your credentials with colleagues.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

If an attacker manages to steal your password via a database breach or phishing page, Multi-Factor Authentication is your second line of defense. It acts as an additional lock on your account.

The Three Security Factors

1. Something You Know: Your Master Password, PIN, or security question answers.
2. Something You Have: Your smartphone (authenticator apps, SMS), security fob, or USB hardware key.
3. Something You Are: Biometrics like fingerprint scans, face recognition, or iris scans.
Critical Warning: If you receive an unsolicited MFA verification prompt on your smartphone while not actively logging in, click Deny immediately and report it. It means an attacker has your password.
