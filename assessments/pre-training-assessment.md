# Pre-Training Assessment: Baseline Security Quiz

This assessment is designed to establish a baseline of security awareness within the organization before formal training begins.

**Instructions for Employees:**
*   Answer all 15 questions to the best of your ability.
*   Do not look up answers or consult colleagues. This is to help us understand our current strength areas and training needs.

---

## Part 1: Phishing & Email Security

### Question 1
What is the primary identifier of a phishing email?
*   A) It always contains spelling errors.
*   B) It mimics a legitimate sender to trick you into taking action.
*   C) It is always sent from an external domain with a `.zip` attachment.
*   D) It is marked as high priority by the mail server.

### Question 2
You receive an email from "HR Benefits" (`hr-benefits@internal-company-announcements.com`) asking you to verify your direct deposit details. Our official company email domain is `ourcompany.com`. What should you do?
*   A) Click the link and update your banking details.
*   B) Report the email immediately as suspicious.
*   C) Reply to the email asking if it is legitimate.
*   D) Delete the email and ignore it.

### Question 3
If you hover your mouse cursor over a link in a suspicious email and the pop-up text displays `http://login.secure-microsoft-portal.ru/index.html`, which part of the URL indicates it is likely a phishing link?
*   A) `microsoft`
*   B) `index.html`
*   C) `login`
*   D) `secure-microsoft-portal.ru`

### Question 4
An email claiming to be from the IT Security Team says that a critical vulnerability has been detected on your machine and you must run the attached file `patch.exe` immediately. What is the safest response?
*   A) Double-click `patch.exe` to secure your machine.
*   B) Forward the email to your teammates so they can secure their machines.
*   C) Do not run the attachment. Contact the IT Service Desk directly via a known phone extension or report the email.
*   D) Save the file to your desktop and run it later.

### Question 5
Which psychological trigger is an attacker using when they send an email warning that your bank account will be closed "by 5:00 PM today" if you do not verify your details?
*   A) Urgency
*   B) Curiosity
*   C) Greed
*   D) Liking

## Part 2: Password Hygiene & Credentials

### Question 6
Which of the following describes the most secure password or passphrase construction?
*   A) `M1cros0ft!` (10 characters, complex)
*   B) `correct-horse-battery-staple` (28 characters, random words)
*   C) `P@ssw0rd2026` (12 characters, complex)
*   D) `mybirthday1995` (14 characters, numbers)

### Question 7
Why is reusing the same password for both your corporate login and your personal social media accounts a high risk?
*   A) It uses too much memory in your browser vault.
*   B) A data breach at the social media company could give attackers your corporate credentials.
*   C) Corporate IT filters can detect personal passwords and block your account.
*   D) It causes synchronization errors in Microsoft Outlook.

### Question 8
What is the primary function of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?
*   A) To speed up the website login process.
*   B) To encrypt files saved on your hard drive.
*   C) To add a secondary layer of security, blocking access even if an attacker has your password.
*   D) To track employee browsing habits.

### Question 9
If you receive an MFA push notification on your phone out of the blue, but you are not actively trying to log into any company system, what should you do?
*   A) Approve it to clear the notification from your screen.
*   B) Deny the request immediately and report the event to the Security Team.
*   C) Wait 15 minutes and see if it happens again.
*   D) Approve it, then change your password later in the week.

### Question 10
What makes a password manager a secure tool for storing credentials?
*   A) It stores passwords in plain text for easy viewing.
*   B) It encrypts your credentials and can generate unique, strong passwords for every account.
*   C) It automatically reports phishing sites to the FBI.
*   D) It shares passwords with your team members automatically.

## Part 3: Social Engineering & Physical Security

### Question 11
What is pretexting in social engineering?
*   A) Creating a fake story or identity to manipulate someone into sharing data or access.
*   B) Sending text messages to target cell phones (Smishing).
*   C) Leaving malware-infected USB sticks in public office spaces.
*   D) Writing fake negative reviews about a company online.

### Question 12
You are entering a secure building using your access badge. An unknown person holding a coffee cup walks behind you and asks you to hold the door. What is the correct protocol?
*   A) Let them in because holding doors is polite.
*   B) Tell them to wait, and call building security.
*   C) Politely request that they scan their own badge first, or direct them to the guest reception.
*   D) Grab the coffee cup to help them, then let them in.

### Question 13
An attacker leaves a USB drive labeled "Executive Salary Matrix" on a table in the office breakroom. What is this tactic called?
*   A) Baiting
*   B) Tailgating
*   C) Spear phishing
*   D) Pretexting

### Question 14
A caller claiming to be from "Microsoft Office Support" calls your phone and asks you to read back the 6-digit PIN that was texted to you so they can verify your account. What should you do?
*   A) Read them the PIN immediately to fix your account.
*   B) Ask them to call back on a different number.
*   C) Refuse to read the code, end the call, and report it to IT Security.
*   D) Read the PIN, but change your password immediately after.

### Question 15
What is the purpose of a "Clean Desk Policy"?
*   A) To ensure janitorial staff can clean the desks easily.
*   B) To reduce clutter and improve workspace ergonomics.
*   C) To secure confidential papers, badges, and devices from unauthorized viewing or physical theft.
*   D) To save paper by forcing digital workflows.
