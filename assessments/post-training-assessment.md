# Post-Training Assessment: Comprehensive Security Quiz

This assessment is designed to measure your cybersecurity awareness after completing the training modules.

**Instructions for Employees:**
*   Answer all 15 questions to the best of your ability.
*   Once completed, submit this form to your training coordinator or complete it via your learning management system (LMS).

---

## Part 1: Phishing & Email Security

### Question 1
An email states that you have been selected for a "Surprise Quarterly Bonus" and includes a link to log in and confirm your banking details. What is the psychological trigger being used here?
*   A) Fear
*   B) Greed/Reward
*   C) Authority
*   D) Urgency

### Question 2
You receive an email from "UPS Shipments" (`delivery-notification@ups-track-package.net`). It asks you to download a shipping receipt `.zip` file to confirm your delivery address. You did not order a package. What is the safest action?
*   A) Double-click the `.zip` file to see what it is.
*   B) Unzip the file using antivirus software first.
*   C) Do not download the file. Mark the email as junk and report it.
*   D) Forward it to your personal email to check it at home.

### Question 3
You hover over a hyperlink in an email from "Adobe Acrobat". The displayed text says `www.adobe.com/sign`, but the hover preview says `http://adobe.pdf-reader-update-portal.info/login`. What does this indicate?
*   A) It is a secure sub-page of Adobe.
*   B) It is a phishing link pointing to an external domain.
*   C) It is a temporary cache page that is safe to access.
*   D) Adobe has updated its official website domain.

### Question 4
An email appears to come from your direct supervisor asking you to send a spreadsheet of employee tax IDs immediately because they are offline. The email is sent from `boss.name.company@gmail.com`. What is the correct response?
*   A) Send the spreadsheet immediately to avoid delaying your supervisor.
*   B) Reply to the email asking why they are using a Gmail address.
*   C) Do not send the data. Contact your supervisor directly through a trusted channel (like phone or in-person) to verify.
*   D) Upload the file to OneDrive and email them the sharing link.

### Question 5
Which of the following is the most reliable way to check the true sender of an email?
*   A) Trust the display name shown in the "From" header.
*   B) Check the sender's email domain (the part after the `@` symbol) and match it to their official company domain.
*   C) Read the signature block at the bottom of the email.
*   D) Check if the email contains a corporate logo image.

## Part 2: Password Hygiene & Credentials

### Question 6
Which of the following passphrases is constructed using the most secure method recommended in training?
*   A) `To-Be-Or-Not-To-Be-1!` (Common quote, 18 characters)
*   B) `guitar-running-coffee-ocean` (4 random words, 27 characters)
*   C) `MyCatFluffy2010!` (Personal details, 16 characters)
*   D) `P@ssword1234` (Predictable pattern, 12 characters)

### Question 7
If a hacker steals your password for a generic online forum, and you have reused that password on your corporate account, what type of attack are you vulnerable to?
*   A) Phishing
*   B) Ransomware
*   C) Credential Stuffing
*   D) DDoS Attack

### Question 8
Which of the following represents the MOST secure Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) method?
*   A) SMS (text message) codes
*   B) Email verification codes
*   C) Hardware security key (e.g., YubiKey)
*   D) A 4-digit security PIN

### Question 9
You log into a work app and receive a notification on your smartphone app asking you to approve the login. You did not prompt this login. What should you do?
*   A) Tap "Approve" so the system doesn't log you out on other machines.
*   B) Tap "Deny/Reject", change your password immediately, and report the attempt to the Security Team.
*   C) Ignore the prompt and let it time out.
*   D) Restart your phone to clear the notification.

### Question 10
How does using an approved password manager help protect your password hygiene?
*   A) It stores all passwords in a secure, encrypted vault and generates unique passwords for every site.
*   B) It allows you to share all passwords with any web user.
*   C) It automatically resets your passwords every 30 days.
*   D) It bypasses MFA requirements on most sites.

## Part 3: Social Engineering & Physical Security

### Question 11
A caller claiming to be from "Company Bank Services" states there is a fraud alert on the corporate account and requests that you read them the bank account number and routing code to block it. What should you do?
*   A) Give them the numbers to prevent fraud.
*   B) Ask them to hold while you look up the numbers, then read them.
*   C) Decline, hang up, and call the bank using the official number listed on your corporate statement or bank card.
*   D) Transfer the call to another department.

### Question 12
An unknown delivery driver carrying a clipboard walks behind you through a badge-secured door and says, "Delivery for the second floor, thanks!" How should you handle this?
*   A) Hold the door open and tell them where the elevator is.
*   B) Ask them to wait at the door and direct them to receptionist/sign-in area so their visit can be verified.
*   C) Check their clipboard, and if it looks authentic, let them in.
*   D) Let them in but notify your manager.

### Question 13
An attacker drops a USB drive labeled "Confidential Staff Layoffs Q4" in the parking lot. An employee finds it and plugs it into a workstation. What is this type of attack called?
*   A) Pretexting
*   B) Baiting
*   C) Vishing
*   D) Tailgating

### Question 14
Which of the following is a physical security action that protects company data when you step away from your desk?
*   A) Placing your laptop in sleep mode without locking.
*   B) Locking your monitor screen with `Windows Key + L` (or equivalent) and putting sensitive documents in a locked drawer.
*   C) Throwing all confidential documents into the standard recycling bin.
*   D) Leaving your workspace badge on your desk next to your keyboard.

### Question 15
If a visitor in the office is not wearing a required guest badge or employee ID card, what is the best security practice?
*   A) Ignore them, assuming someone else verified them.
*   B) Follow them around the office silently.
*   C) Politely ask if they need help finding reception or the person they are visiting, or notify security/HR.
*   D) Ask them to leave the building immediately without explanation.
